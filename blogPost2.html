The Mercator Trade Platform: Laying the Foundations for Smarter Cross Border Trade

Global trade corridors are the lifeblood of commerce, yet many border crossings still rely heavily on paper-based processes, fragmented systems, and manual checks. The Mercator Trade Platform (MTP) aims to change that by providing a single, trusted digital hub and gateway for customs, payments, and compliance.

Thoughtful foundations, not a leap in the dark

From the outset, MTP has been informed by extensive research into existing regional infrastructure, standards, and workflows. Rather than starting with unproven concepts, we have examined:

  • Customs Modernisation Programmes in Southern Africa, which already align national systems to international frameworks.
  • The WCO Data Model (World Customs Organisation’s global data dictionary) and UN/CEFACT (United Nations Centre for Trade Facilitation and Electronic Business) code lists, ensuring that every field—from HS (Harmonised System) codes to port identifiers—speaks the same language.
  • Emerging payment systems such as the Pan African Payment and Settlement System (PAPSS), which offers real-time, multi-currency settlement aligned to ISO 20022 (the international financial messaging standard).

Cloud-native and API-first approach

MTP is not a single monolithic application but a suite of microservices, each offering a well-defined API (application programming interface). This “API-first” philosophy means:

  • Rapid integration with existing customs systems (via lightweight adaptors), avoiding costly rip-and-replace projects.
  • Continuous delivery—new features, security patches, and improvements can be deployed in minutes, not months, thanks to containerisation, infrastructure as code, and GitOps workflows.
  • Scalability and resilience, with services running in public cloud regions close to customs authorities and mirrored across providers for high availability.

Practical pilots, measured steps

Rather than revealing every detail at once, MTP will begin with an initial pilot focusing on the core “green lane” workflow: electronic submission of a customs declaration, digital “release token” issuance and same day, multi currency duty settlement. This will demonstrate tangible benefits—reduced queues, fewer errors and faster payments—before expanding to additional services such as health and phytosanitary certificates, risk analytics and machine learning driven forecasting.

Collaboration and standards leadership

MTP is conceived as a central trust anchor—a not for profit entity bringing together customs administrations, trade facilitation banks, logistics providers and inspection bodies. By contributing real world use cases and data to ongoing standards efforts (for example, extensions to the WCO Data Model and JSON schemas for trade finance guarantees), MTP seeks to shape the very foundations of digital trade facilitation.

Next steps

Over the coming months, we will engage with pilot partners to refine our data dictionary, finalise API specifications and stand up a live sandbox environment. Stay tuned for updates as we move from research to reality, demonstrating that smarter, standards based digital trade is within reach—without disruptive overhauls or untested technologies.
